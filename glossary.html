Speak the CISO's language and understand the modern threat landscape.

01 General Cybersecurity Concepts

Zero Trust Architecture

A security model that assumes every person—even a "trusted" candidate or employee—must have their identity verified every single time they try to access company systems.

Initial Access

The first "footprint" a hacker makes in your company network, which nowadays often happens when a criminal poses as a job seeker to get their foot in the door.

Insider Threat

The risk that someone who already has "the keys to the building"—like a current employee or a fraudulent new hire—will accidentally or intentionally cause a data breach.

IAM Framework

Identity & Access Management: The digital "gatekeeper" system that ensures only the right people have access to specific HR tools (like payroll or employee records) based on their job role.

SOC 2 / ISO 27001

These are "gold standard" security certifications that prove a company handles sensitive candidate and employee data with the highest level of care.

Data Sovereignty

The legal requirement to follow the specific data privacy laws (like GDPR) of the country where an employee actually lives and works.

Zero-Knowledge Proof

A clever privacy technology that allows a recruiter to confirm a candidate is telling the truth (like verifying they have a degree) without actually seeing or storing their private documents.

CMMC Standard

A set of strict cybersecurity rules required by the government to ensure any company—including their HR department—is secure enough to handle sensitive federal or defense-related information.

02 Candidate Fraud Specifics

Deepfake Identity

Using AI-generated video or audio to impersonate a real person or create a completely "fake" face during a remote interview.

Synthetic Identity Fraud

A "Frankenstein" identity created by combining real stolen data (like a Social Security number) with fake information (like a new name and address).

Proxy Interviewing

A bait-and-switch tactic where a highly skilled professional secretly takes the technical interview on behalf of a less-qualified candidate.

Resume Padding

AI-Enhanced: Using AI tools to automatically generate perfect-fit experience, fake certifications, and falsified employment history that bypasses standard filters.

Virtual Backgrounds

Concealment: Using digital backgrounds or "looping" videos during an interview to hide the fact that the candidate is actually in a different country or a "click farm" office.

Footprint Mismatch

Digital: When a candidate's professional online presence (like LinkedIn or GitHub) doesn't exist or doesn't match the experience they claim on their application.

Social Engineering

For Onboarding: A scam where a "new hire" tries to convince HR to change their direct deposit info or ship high-end laptop equipment to a different address before their start date.

Credential Stuffing

HR Edition: When a fraudster uses a stolen list of usernames and passwords from other data breaches to try and break into your company’s specific Applicant Tracking System (ATS).
